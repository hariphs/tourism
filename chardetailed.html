charminar

The Charminar is a historic monument located in Hyderabad, India, and is one of the city's most iconic landmarks. Built in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the founder of Hyderabad, it was constructed to commemorate the eradication of a deadly plague that had ravaged the region. The Charminar, meaning "Four Minarets," is a stunning example of Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring four grand arches and towering minarets that stand at the intersection of Hyderabad's old city. It is not only a symbol of the city's rich history but also a bustling hub for markets and culture today.
