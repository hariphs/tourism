Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a world-famous monument of white marble built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed between 1632 and 1653, it is renowned for its architectural beauty and intricate craftsmanship, blending elements of Islamic, Persian, and Indian styles. The central dome, minarets, and the exquisite inlay work of semi-precious stones make it a masterpiece. The Taj Mahal is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is often regarded as a symbol of love and eternal beauty.
