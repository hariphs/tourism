Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a spiritual and cultural landmark located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the most revered site in Sikhism and symbolizes equality and humility. The temple, renowned for its stunning architecture and gold-plated exterior, is built around a man-made pool of water, called the Amrit Sarovar, where pilgrims bathe. The holy scripture, Guru Granth Sahib, is recited continuously within the temple. Open to people of all faiths, the Golden Temple emphasizes the Sikh principles of community service and devotion through its free kitchen, or langar, which serves meals to thousands daily.
